üéØ What We Do

Real Adventures, Real Skills, Real Character

Our troop has built an impressive campsite featuring a commanding 30-foot flagpole where Old Glory flies high. We've conducted solemn flag retirement ceremonies honoring our nation's colors, built and launched model rockets skyward, and conquered trails at both Ricketts Glen and Worlds End State Parks.

Around crackling campfires and with cast iron Dutch ovens, we master the art of outdoor cooking. We've constructed a working rope-making machine and are learning essential knots and ropework. At our dedicated campsite, boys test their aim at our archery and BB rifle range, practice hatchet throwing, and engage in pugil stick combat training. Through hands-on first aid instruction and biblical character training, we forge the next generation of American patriots and Christian warriors.

üéØ Advancement & Awards

Woodlands Trail Program (Ages 5-10)

Our youngest warriors earn recognition through the Branch Patch, Forest Award, and Worthy Life Award. Boys earn Branch Pins by completing requirements in seven branches of activities and receive the Forest Award for mastering all branches within their age group (Fox, Hawk, or Mountain Lion).

Highest Honor: The Timberline Award signifies a Mountain Lion's readiness to advance to the Navigators program, earned after completing the Mountain Lion Forest Award and bridging activities.

Special Recognition: Sylvan Stars (additional Forest Award achievements), Woodsmen Award (knife safety mastery), and the faith-building Worthy Life Award.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should my son bring to meetings?

Boys should wear their Trail Life uniform (or regular clothes until they receive their uniform), bring a water bottle, notebook, and pen. Specific activities may require additional items - we'll notify you in advance.

How much does it cost?

Youth Membership: $42.27 per year (paid directly to Trail Life USA)
Adult Membership: $46.55 per year (covers background checks and youth protection training)

Additional costs may include uniforms, handbooks, and troop activity fees. We actively fundraise to minimize costs for families. All fees are paid via credit or debit card and are non-refundable.

Can I volunteer even if my son isn't a member?

We welcome adult volunteers! All volunteers must complete a volunteer application and background check. Contact us to learn about opportunities to serve.

What age groups do you serve?

Foxes: Ages 5-6
Hawks: Ages 7-8
Mountain Lions: Ages 9-10
Navigators: Ages 11-13
Adventurers: Ages 14-18

Each level has age-appropriate activities, challenges, and advancement opportunities designed specifically for that stage of development.
